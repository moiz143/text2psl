Pakistan has its own Sign Language called Pakistan Sign Language (PSL). A noteworthy population of deaf people live in Pakistan, there is a need for computer aided translator that can translate voice or text to PSL. Such tools can facilitate deaf in their communication and education. This project proposed a computer aided Translator called PakParse, which takes text (English & Urdu) and voice as input, and animate corresponding signs by creating 3D avatar.


PAKISTAN SIGN LANGUAGE

Every country has their unique sign languages. Pakistan sign language is gesture language having its syntax and vocabulary, which is majorly inspired by Urdu, the national language of Pakistan. These signs are further classified into manual and non-manual signs. Manual signs consist of hand shapes, arm and body movements, and their locations. Whereas non-manual signs include facial expressions, gazing, shoulder raising, and head movements.


The Translation Mechanism

PSL DICTIONARY is inspired by two languages(Urdu and English) as input and play animation according to it. First of all preprocessing is applied on the input, then its relevant HamNoSys code is fetched from database. This code is helpful for generating SiGML which is an XML and used to create animation frames for the avatar.
